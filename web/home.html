Aging • Health Talks• Apr 6th '24

Successful Aging

Join us as Sandy introduces Dr. Ronald D. Adelman, Co-Chief of the Center on Aging, and Chief of the Division of Geriatrics and Palliative Medicine at Weill Cornell Medicine. This week's health talk will focus on Dr. Adleman's career path and his years as a geriatrician. Dr. Adelman will talk about why he believes people of any age can make new friendships and reduce their loneliness and isolation. He will talk about what prompted him to create a Wellness Program at the Center on Aging and why he thinks it is so important for Wellness to be a component of geriatric care, now and through the aging process.

Discovering • mindfulness with sarah waxse• Jul 5th '24

An Exercise in Tranquility

Join us for an exercise in tranquility. Mindfulness is a type of meditation in which you focus on being intensely aware of what you're sensing and feeling in the moment, without interpretation or judgment. Sarah Waxse, Licensed Clinical Social Worker at NewYork Presbyterian Hospital, is a Mindfulness expert. She studied with Jon Kabat-Zinn, the founder of mindfulness stress reduction, and has offered Mindfulness classes throughout NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital and New York City. Sarah’s soothing voice and easy direction will help relieve your stress and brighten your day.

Eating • What's Cooking with Joanna Pruess? • Jul 5th '24

Fish is the Dish for May

Joanna Pruess is a food and travel writer who has written for The New York Times Magazine, The Washington Post, The Associated Press, and numerous other publications. Her fifteen cookbooks include Cast-Iron Cooking for Two, Soup for Two: Small-Batch Recipes for One, Two, or a Few; The Tea Cookbook; The Cast-Iron Cookbook; and Seduced by Bacon: Recipes and Lore about America’s Favorite Indulgence. Best of all, she is a patient of our Center on Aging geriatric practice! She is eager to bring her expertise and passion to our Wellness Program, with Zoom cooking demonstration classes and other interactive and creative experiences. This merry month of May, Joanna will focus on fish dishes that are easily created and are delicious, heart healthy, and sure to impress. And a special dessert treat that is also heart healthy and sure to satisfy your sweet tooth. Join us for this mouth-watering session.

Connecting • Improve is For Everyone• Jul 5th '24

You Can Do This

Dr. Elaina Dellacava, the Center on Aging GeroPsychiatrist, has a great way to unleash your creativity, learn new skills and meet new people. It’s called Improvisation. You may think you don’t have an acting bone in your body, but that is not what this Improv is about. Dr. Dellacava honed her Improv skills at NYC’s Improvolution, an Improv school “that trains and inspires people so they build the confidence they need to evolve.” You may not win an Oscar or a Tony, but we are sure you will gain confidence, find new ways to express yourself and free yourself to have fun. This will be a four-class session, starting on May 23rd and then repeating weekly for three more sessions, on the fourth Thursday of the month: May 30, June 6, and June 13. This is a closed group, and you will need to sign up by emailing the address below.

About Wellness

The Wellness Program’s mission is to promote wellness for the Center on Aging’s patients. Inspired by those around him, Dr Ron Adelman, Executive Direct or of the Center on Aging, conceptualized a platform where patients could feel less isolated by making social connections with one another and providers. By offering educational programs on food and nutrition, exercise, cultural enrichment and more, we hope participants will meet new people, make new friends, and learn new things.
