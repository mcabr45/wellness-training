Fascinating Facts from History

If you are a history buff, we have a site for you. The History Facts website sends daily pieces of little-known historical information to your tablet, phone or desktop. Amaze your family and friends with little known tidbits. For instance, did you know Ohio and Michigan went to war in 1835? How about the fact that Andrew Jackson’s pet parrot was kicked out of the President’s funeral for swearing? Each day, History Facts publishes a daily digest highlighting a lesser-known event from yesteryear, one your textbooks probably did not cover, but you are likely to enjoy learning about. You need to subscribe, but it is free. Subscribe to receive the daily digest along with featured articles that shed even more light on fascinating moments and people from times gone by.

East Side Feed

I Love the Upper West Side

Do you live on the Upper West Side? Or do you visit it frequently? This is an important website for you to know about. "I Love the Upper West Side.com" link will take you to a very handy newsletter that can be delivered to your email address on a regular basis, for FREE. It is chock full of up-to-date information on all things Upper West Side – new restaurants, the closing of old favorites, neighborhood news, shopping, real estate. Just follow the link below and sign up for the free newsletter. Then stay informed about your favorite neighborhood and all it has to offer.

Girls Travel Virtual Tours

Girl Travel Tours is a wonderful way to see the world without leaving your chair or couch. The tour guides are native to the countries they guide and are full of tidbits about little known facts and sites. These virtual tours include beautiful photos and wonderful information, and there is a Question-and-Answer session at the end. Who knows? This is The Apollo Theater

“Since opening its doors in 1914 and introducing the first Amateur Night contests in 1934, the Apollo has played a major role in the emergence of jazz, swing, bebop, R&B, gospel, blues, and soul — all quintessentially American music genres. Ella Fitzgerald, Sarah Vaughan, Billie Holiday, Sammy Davis Jr., James Brown, Gladys Knight, Luther Vandross, D’Angelo, Lauryn Hill, and countless others began their road to stardom on the Apollo stage. Today, the Apollo is a respected not-for-profit, presenting concerts, theatrical and dance performances, film screenings, education and community outreach programs.” In 1914, Benjamin Hurtig and Harry Seamon obtained a thirty-year lease on the newly constructed theater calling it Hurtig and Seamon’s New Burlesque Theater. Like many American theaters during this time, African Americans were not allowed to attend as patrons or to perform. Today, the Apollo presents concerts, performing arts, education, and community outreach programs that “build on the cultural heritage of Harlem and celebrate the enormous well of creativity found in our neighborhood.” If you cannot go there in person, look at some of what the Apollo Digital Library offers in virtual programs, performances, and educational resources.

Snug Harbor is for the Birds

Enjoy a short video explaining the benefits of birding for people of all ages. It is a low-cost activity, and an opportunity to explore nature and meet people with a similar interest. The Wellness Program plans to introduce you to exhibits and activities throughout the five boroughs of NYC, and here we are focusing on Staten Island’s Snug Harbor, which has created this informative video on birding. Snap up your binoculars and walking shoes and head on over. And if birds are not your thing, Snug Harbor has many other programs you may be interested in. Staten Island here we come!
