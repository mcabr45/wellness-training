NYCares Volunteer Opportunities

If you are like many of the Center on Aging’s patients, you may be retired, or have children and grandchildren who live far away. You may be looking for rewarding ways to spend your time. What could be more rewarding than volunteering your time to help others? But how do you find volunteer opportunities you may be interested in? You may know of NY Cares from their yearly seasonal coat drives, but there is so much more that you can do yearround– it is a one stop shop of Volunteering opportunities: education, food prep, sports, senior support and many more areas of interest. You can access their website, create a free account, and you will be on your way to finding fulfilling volunteer opportunities.

Get to Know Your Neighbors

If you live on the Upper West Side, we have an organization that you should know about. Bloomingdale Aging in Place (BAiP) is an association of friends and neighbors whose mission is to help older adults lead vital, connected, safe and comfortable lives as their needs change. An all-volunteer, intergenerational network of neighbors, BAiP builds community by organizing social activities, sharing information, and extending a helping hand to neighbors as they continue to age at home. (BAiP is free to join for residents of the UWS neighborhood bounded by W. 96th - 110th Streets.) Take a look at their website – it may open you up to new connections.

Connecting • Learn Something New!

Dare to Play Pickleball

It is the fastest growing sport in America. Pickleball is for all ages, and all skill levels. It is a great way to exercise, develop skills, and meet new people – or meet regularly with old friends. There are pickleball courts all over NYC, you just need to know where to find them. That is why we want to give you a little direction. Take a look at the NYC park website to find all the publicly available pickleball courts around the city (You can also find additional facilities on the NYC pickleball website.) Experience how much fun pickleball can be and remember, you are never too old to learn something new!

SAGE for LGBTQ+ Seniors

If you are an LGBTQ+ older adult (or an ally) you should know about SAGE. SAGE has been serving the LGBTQ+ for more than 30 years. It is estimated that by 2030 there will be approximately 7 million LGBTQ+ people in the U.S. who are 50 and older. LGBTQ+ older people face unique challenges as they age. LGBTQ+ elders are twice as likely to be single and live alone; four times less likely to have children; far more likely to have faced discrimination and social stigma. They are more likely to face poverty and homelessness, and to have poor health. “But even as we face these challenges, we are resilient. LGBTQ+ older people are living vibrant, full lives across the U.S. and around the world.” Check out their website at the address below. SAGE has numerous resources that may be of interest to you, including volunteer opportunities.

DOROT USA

DOROT is the Hebrew word for “generations." DOROT was founded by social workers who had recently graduated Columbia University’s social work program. They were “pioneers” in their approach to serving their older neighbors with the goal of alleviating social isolation. Recognizing the plight of elders hidden in their apartments on New York City’s Upper West Side, they began with regular visits and food deliveries. These young adults forged a loving bond between the generations, fulfilling the name they chose for their organization. Though it retains its Jewish roots, DOROT’s reach has expanded to serve and welcome people of all faiths and cultures. Programs engage 10,000 seniors and volunteers annually. DOROT serves the Jewish and wider community, bringing the generations together in a mutually beneficial partnership of elders, volunteers, and professionals. Now serving Manhattan, Westchester and Queens.
